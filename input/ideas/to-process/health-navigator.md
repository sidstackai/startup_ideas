# Idea 2: SaltSwitch (The Health Ally Ecosystem)
1. The Core Concept
A "Patient-First" navigation layer that sits between the doctor's prescription and the pharmacy. It uses AI to decode medical jargon and a proprietary "Salt-Mapping" engine to switch patients from high-margin branded generics to verified, low-cost alternatives.

2. Target Market & ICP
Segment: Chronic patients (Diabetes, Hypertension, Cardiac) and their primary caregivers.

Geography: Tier-1 and Tier-2 India.

User Persona: The "Chief Health Officer" of the family—usually a tech-literate millennial/Gen-Z adult (like Siddharth) managing the recurring ₹5,000 - ₹15,000 monthly medicine bill for their parents.

3. The Problem & JTBD
The JTBD: "Help me ensure my parents are getting the right treatment without being exploited by pharmaceutical brand markups I don't understand."

The Pain: Information asymmetry. Patients don't know that a ₹200 pill and a ₹20 pill have the exact same active ingredient (salt).

The Gap: Existing apps (1mg/Apollo) are marketplaces; they make more money when you buy the expensive brand. They have no incentive to be a "Pure Advocate."

4. The Solution Architecture
The Decoder (OCR + LLM): Scans handwritten or digital prescriptions. It translates medical shorthand into a 3D visual "Recovery Roadmap" explaining exactly what each drug does.

The Trust-Switch Engine: A database that maps branded drugs to WHO-GMP certified generic equivalents, displaying a side-by-side "Efficacy vs. Price" chart.

The Conflict Monitor: An active monitoring layer that checks for contraindications (e.g., "Drug A from your Cardiologist reacts badly with Drug B from your Urologist").

5. "The Enemy"
Primary Enemy: The "Branded Generic" markup—pharma companies spending 30% of revenue on doctor-kickbacks rather than R&D.

Secondary Enemy: Patient confusion. The fear that "Cheaper = Counterfeit."

6. GTM Strategy (Siddharth’s Leverage)
The Wedge: Partner with Corporate HRs (using CXO network) to offer "Health Bill Reduction" as an employee benefit for their aging parents.

The Channel: Content-led trust building. Partnering with non-commissioned medical influencers to demonstrate the "Salt-Switch" science.

7. Business Model & Scale
Pricing: Subscription model (₹499/year) for the "Advocacy" + 10% fulfillment fee on medicine orders.

Potential: 10M chronic patients in India. Capturing 1% = ₹50 Cr ($6M) ARR.
